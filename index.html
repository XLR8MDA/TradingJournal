Stop loss placement
R1
SL goes behind the HUNT TIP, not the S/R line. Add 5–10 pip buffer beyond the furthest wick point.
R2
Never use a round-number SL. If SL falls on 4,700 exactly, move to 4,703 or 4,697.
R3
Max SL: Gold 30–50 pips · BTC $300–600 · EUR/USD 15–25 pips.
Take profit management
TP1
Close 50% at nearest opposing S/R. Move SL to breakeven immediately.
TP2
Let remaining 50% run to next major S/R. Trail SL behind each new swing high/low.
T/O
Time-out: if price stalls 6+ candles without progress, close at market.
Hard exit rules — immediate close, no exceptions

✗
High-impact news fires while in trade
✗
Price reaches major opposing S/R with strong rejection
✗

Position sizing formula
Risk per trade = Account balance × 1%
Lot size = Risk amount ÷ (SL in pips × pip value)
$10,000 × 1% = $100 · SL 40 pips · pip value $0.10 → 0.25 lots

Where you actually enter
4 LTF entry signals
1. Change of Character (ChoCH)Tightest entry
After the 15m hunt, drop to 1m/5m. Wait for the first higher high — this breaks micro structure. Enter on close or retest. Most precise SL placement.
2. LTF engulfing / pin barFast signal
Wait for a pin bar or engulfing at the 15m S/R level on 1m/5m. Enter on its close. Candle low/high = micro SL.
3. Order block limit entryBest R:R
Identify the last bearish candle before the bullish impulse (longs). Limit at the top of that body. SL below its low. No chasing.
Limit orderNo chasing
4. Compression breakoutMomentum
After the 15m hunt, 1m/5m compresses into tight inside bars or doji. Enter the breakout in direction of 15m bias. SL below compression low.

Pattern Catalog
Reversal and continuation setups
Reversal patterns
Pin bar / hammer / shooting starHigh edge
Long wick (2x+ the body) rejecting S/R. The wick = the stop hunt. Enter on close or open of next candle. SL behind wick tip.
Engulfing candleHigh edge
At resistance: bearish engulfs prior bullish. At support: bullish engulfs bearish. SL behind pattern high/low.
Double top / double bottomMedium edge
Two equal highs at resistance / lows at support. Second touch is the hunt. Enter on neckline break.
Tweezer top / bottomMedium edge
Two consecutive candles with equal wick highs/lows at S/R. Signals rejection after micro stop hunt.
Evening / morning starContext edge
3-candle reversal: strong move into S/R → small doji → strong reversal. Best on 15m at a 4H S/R level.
Continuation patterns
Bull / bear flagHigh edge
Sharp impulse, tight angled pullback retesting prior resistance-turned-support. Hunt dips below flag low, then breakout.
Inside bar at S/RHigh edge
After a strong trend candle, next candle fully contained inside it. The inside bar IS the coil. Enter breakout in trend direction.
S/R flip retestHigh edge
Old resistance becomes new support. Price breaks, pulls back, hunts stops below briefly, continues. Most reliable continuation.
Ascending / descending triangleMedium edge
Final touch of support often briefly breaks (hunts stops) before breakout. Enter on reclose above support.

7 Profit Killers
The behaviours that destroy a valid edge
1. OvertradingMost common
Taking 8–10 trades instead of 1–2 quality setups. More trades = more fees, lower win rate.
Fix: Maximum 2 trades per session. If no setup scores ≥12, not trading IS the discipline.
2. Widening SL mid-tradeEdge destroyer
Moving SL further away invalidates the setup logic. The hunt tip defines the SL.
Fix: Set SL at entry. Lock it. Only move to breakeven after TP1 hits.
3. Taking all profit at TP1R:R killer
Closing 100% at TP1 cuts average winner from 3R to 1R.
Fix: Always 50/50 split. Close half at TP1, trail the rest. Trust the plan.
4. Revenge tradingAccount killer
After a loss the brain seeks immediate recovery — oversized, low-quality setups.
Fix: 2-loss daily limit is hard law. After 2 losses, close the platform.
5. No trade journalHidden killer
Without logging you cannot identify patterns in your own mistakes. You repeat the same errors for months.
Fix: Log every trade. After 30 trades, review: which patterns lose? Which sessions?
6. Jumping between strategiesConsistency killer
Switching after 3 losses means no strategy ever gets a fair sample size.
Fix: Commit for minimum 50 trades. A 3-trade losing streak is normal at 86% win rate.
7. Risking too much too soonMost fatal
5-trade losing streak at 5% = 23% drawdown. The account never recovers psychologically.
Fix: Start at 0.5–1% regardless of account size. Scale after 3 consecutive profitable months.
Trader Phases
The 4 stages every trader goes through
Phase 1 · Months 1–3
Learning
Losing money while building pattern recognition. Goal is NOT profitability — it is executing the checklist correctly on 80%+ of trades. Use demo or micro lots.
Target: Process discipline. Never: P&L targets.
Phase 2 · Months 3–6
Consistency
Breaking even or small losses. Journal becomes critical — shows exactly where you break the rules. Fix one flaw per month.
Target: 3 consecutive weeks without breaking a rule.
Phase 3 · Months 6–12
Profitability
Consistently profitable months. Rules are now habits. Scale gradually: 0.5% → 1% → 1.5% risk.
Target: 3 consecutive profitable months. WR ≥65%, R:R ≥1:2.5.
Phase 4 · Month 12+
Scaling
Scale to 2% max risk. All 3 instruments active. Monthly target: 5–15% compounded.
Target: 15%+ annual return. Withdrawals begin.
Daily Routine
10-step process from prep to post-session
Pre-session · 30 min before London or NY open
01
Check economic calendar. Mark all red news events. No trades 30 min before/after.
02
Map S/R on 4H and 1H for all 3 instruments. Takes 10 minutes.
03
Identify which S/R levels price is closest to — these are watch zones for today.
04
Set price alerts at watch zones. Do NOT sit watching charts. Wait for the alert.
During session · When alert fires
05
Open chart. Run the 15m checklist. Score the setup. Only continue if ≥12/16.
06
Drop to 1m/5m for LTF entry. Identify ChoCH, engulfing, order block, or compression.
07
Enter trade. Set SL behind hunt tip +5 pips. Set TP1 and TP2 as limit orders immediately.
08
Close the chart. Let the trade run. Only check when TP1 or SL alert fires.
Post-session · 15 min
09
Log every trade in the journal — win or loss, all fields.
10
Ask: "Did I follow every rule?" If yes — good session regardless of P&L.
Weekly review · Sunday · 30 min
W1
Count trades, wins, losses, avg R:R. Is the edge showing?
W2
Tag every trade where a rule was broken. Find the most common break.
W3
Identify which instrument and session produced the best results.
W4
Set one specific behavioural goal for next week.
W5
Re-map key S/R levels for the coming week on all 3 instruments.
Mindset Rules
10 principles that separate profitable traders
01
You are a probability operator, not a predictor. Your job is to execute a positive-EV process, not to be right on every trade.
02
A loss following the rules is a good trade. A win breaking the rules is dangerous. Judge on process, not outcome.
03
Boredom is a signal you are trading correctly. If nothing is happening, you are waiting for quality. That is exactly right.
04
Never think about money while in a trade. Think only in R units. Money attachment causes premature exits.
05
The market owes you nothing. Each setup is independent. Treat it that way.
06
Missed trades are not losses. Waiting for the checklist is discipline, not failure.
07
Drawdowns are part of the system. A 3-trade losing run happens every ~20 trades at 86% win rate. Accept it before it happens.
08
Confidence comes from preparation, not winning. If you backtested and ran the checklist — be confident regardless of the last result.
09
Separate trading from living expenses. Never trade money you need. Financial pressure warps every decision.
10
Progress is measured in months, not days. Three bad months in a row is the only real data point that matters.
The honest truth: The edge is real and backtested. The only thing standing between you and consistent profitability is whether you execute the process with discipline — on good days, bad days, after losses, and after wins.
